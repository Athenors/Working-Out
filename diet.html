DIET IS VERY IMPORTANT!!!!

1. Make sure to hit calorific goals

Whether you're trying to gain/lose weight or maintain, getting the sufficient calories is necessary for proper brain and body functioning. According to the nhs women should intake 2k calories a day while men should intake 2.5k. Tracking calories is very useful and I suggest using an application such as myfitnesspal.

2. Eat after you exercise

Make sure to eat after you exercise so that your body can use the nutrients for your muscles to recover. It is recommended that you eat a meal high in protein and carbohydrates within 2 hours.

3. Eat Snacks!

The majority of people can enjoy a few little snacks before and after exercise. How you feel is important. Do what suits you best. Snacks consumed shortly before exercise are unlikely to provide you with extra energy if your session is less than 60 minutes, but they may help you avoid disorienting hunger sensations. If your workout lasts longer than 60 minutes, it may be beneficial to consume something high in carbohydrates before, during, or after the activity. Suitable snack choices include:

  • Energy Bar
  • Fruits such as a Banana
